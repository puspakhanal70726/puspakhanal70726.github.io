Friday, November 24, 2017

My Contributing Experience

I understand that contributing to open source is about learning and sharing ideas with other people. When I first heard about contributing to open source, I thought that it only involved coding related to the software, but that is not the case. We can contribute to anything, on any topic, if we have an idea about the topic. Contributing means giving something to the tech community and gaining experience in how to work with other people in the real world. People can also contribute on literally any social media when they have an open-source license and if they have great knowledge about the project.

My contribution to the open source project is fixing coding and spelling errors based on my knowledge and capacity. When I contribute on open source, I am sure to meet people with more experience and improve my skills thanks to their feedback. Contributing to open source means becoming familiar with other people, knowing each other, and getting each other. Contribute to open source: to become familiar with other people, to improve my knowledge of how to work in the real world, to share ideas, and to get the feedback I need so that I can improve and be stronger on the field next time.

The best way to get familiar with the project is to read the repository including the license, README file, Contributing file, Code of Conduct, and other documentation. After I read all those files, I found a resource where I could find open-source projects. Among those, I found that Open Source Friday is a good resource for open-source projects. The most important part is that the contribution must be meaningful to the project, and if we get a positive response, that means you have gained some level of experience. A positive response always encourages us to do more projects and help the tech community better their ways. One of my friends posted on Facebook that his PC fan had broken down and he did not know how to fix it. When I saw that message, I sent him a link to a website where we could buy a fan online or find instructions about how to fix his PC fan by himself. Sharing is love, and sharing gives us experience, and experience gives us confidence, and confidence leads to success, and success makes you happy.
